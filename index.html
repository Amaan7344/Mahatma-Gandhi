Early Life

Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, a town in Gujarat, India. His full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. His father, Karamchand Gandhi, was a respected political leader, and his mother, Putlibai, was very religious and kind. Gandhi was a quiet and shy child who enjoyed listening to stories from Hindu epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. These stories taught him values like truth and non-violence. He started his education in Porbandar and later studied in Rajkot. Although he was not a brilliant student, he was very honest, disciplined, and respectful. His early life shaped his strong moral character, which later guided his work for India‚Äôs freedom.

Philosophy

üìö Influences on His Philosophy

    1. Satya (Truth)

    Gandhi believed that truth is God. Living truthfully meant being honest in thoughts, words, and actions.

    2. Ahimsa (Non-Violence)

    Ahimsa meant non-violence in action, thought, and speech. Gandhi called it the greatest force of mankind.

    3. Satyagraha (Insistence on Truth)

    It was his method of non-violent protest against injustice. It required courage, self-control, and moral strength.

    4. Sarvodaya (Welfare of All)

    Gandhi believed in upliftment of all people, not just the privileged. He dreamed of a just and equal society‚ÄîRam Rajya.

    5. Simple Living, High Thinking

    He led a simple life, wore khadi, and promoted self-reliance, rejecting materialism and luxury.

    6. Religious Tolerance and Harmony

    He respected all religions and believed they all lead to the same truth. He promoted interfaith harmony.

    7. Trustship

    Wealth, he said, should be held in trust for the welfare of the poor. Rich people should use their resources for public good.

    8. Self-Discipline and Self-Purification

    Gandhi practiced fasting, celibacy, and meditation to stay spiritually strong. He believed inner purity brings outer peace.

He was on his way to attend an evening prayer meeting at Birla House when he was shot at point-blank range by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist who opposed Gandhi‚Äôs efforts to promote peace and unity between Hindus and Muslims after Partition.
